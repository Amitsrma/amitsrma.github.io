When I started doing pushups seriously, which is at around 2011, I could hardly do 37 straight. On my thirty-eighth attempt, I remember
my arms were shaking and I could feel the sweat flowing from my forehead to the tip of my nose as 
I would try to push up with shaking arms while the sweat accumulated on the nose tip and dropped, that I followed
dropping flat on my chest.

As the days went by, I persisted and tried my best. These days, I can easily do at least 50 pushups without breaking
a sweat, on my bad day. I would like to share the journey I took to get to where I am now from the point where
I could not even do 38 pushups no matter how hard I tried.

There are a couple of ideas to follow if you want to succeed in being able to increase the number of pushups, and now as I grow older I realize that you can use those ideas in other areas too, like education, work and maybe even relationship (this might be a little stretch but, it is possible). I would like to tell about how my opinion on the process should be like and then
on describing what I did. I was able to achieve the physical prowess in no such short period, it took me around 3 months
to properly grow after I realized what I was doing was ineffective.
            
Following are my opinions:
<ul>
    <li>
        <b>Pick A Time .i.e. Discipline:</b>

You have to set aside a time of day that works best for you. When I say Best For You, I mean you should make sure that that specific time slot is almost always free; if it is not free on occasions, make it free on those occasions too.
I would prefer it if it was after office hours because some times you are happy with what you did at work and you
train better. Sometimes you have frustrations about work and while you are frustrated, you tend to train longer and harder. That's what happens to me at least. Most importantly, training will reduce the frustrations you developed during the work and if you are happy, make you happier. It is hard to find a good time spot and it seems you are wasting precious time doing an unproductive activity, but this activity will increase your productivity.
                    </li>
                    <li>
                        <b>Commit:</b>
    
Now that you have set aside a time, you have to commit to it. No excuses. Just as I mentioned earlier, there will be
frustrations and you will feel like not training, there will be times when you are ecstatic and you want to
not train and go treat yourself with something. Well, I won't say don't enjoy, you can take a day off from your normal
schedule but never more than 2 days off in a row. Once you have taken 2 days off, no off days for the next 2 weeks at least.

            </li>
        </ul> 

You are now ready. If you pick a time of day to train and commit to it, you will see results.

Now, as for pushups, here is what I did.

My initial goal was to be able to do as many pushups in one go as possible. As I mentioned I could not do more than 37 on my first try.
I persisted, I increased by 1 every day. My best increased to 47 in one week. After that, my best started to plateau. 
It did not increase as wildly as the first week for weeks. in the next 2 weeks, I reached 52. I remember my arms were still shaking just as hard as they were when I did the first time however I was sweating less (not sweating a lot felt good).

![barrier](/Image/barrier.jpg){: style="width=90%"}

Seeing as I was not progressing much, I thought I might be doing something wrong. With pushups, my pectoral muscles were developing fast. Towards the end of pushups, I would strain myself a little too hard and I could feel my abs and lats were tightening.

<h3> Change the Plan, Never the Goal</h3>

![](/Image/why.jpeg){: style="width=100%"}

Not seeing much progress, I changed the way I worked out. Instead of focusing on doing a lot of pushups 
I can do in one go or one set, I tried to do some of them in a lot of sets. What do I mean by this? I was able to barely do around 50 pushups straight, right? Now, I changed the way I did it and started doing 4 sets of 35 pushups.
I set the interval between sets to not exceed 120 seconds (believe me, that's plenty of time when you get used to it).

<h3>NEVER GIVE UP</h3>

It was challenging at first but now I was doing 140 pushups instead of 52. It took a lot of time to complete them but once I started I did not stop. If it felt like I might not be able to complete the next repetition, I would
just hold until I felt confident to do next one (which is about 10-15 seconds hold); I was determined to not fall flat on my chest and I was determined to complete 35.


<h3>FRUITS OF NOT GIVING UP</h3>
![](/Image/success.jpg){: style="width:100%"}

As the days went by, I could easily do 4 sets of 35. It was not challenging enough. Now I increased the number of pushups I did
per set to 40, and soon 40 was not challenging enough. Soon, I was doing things like starting my first set with 35 pushups,
continue to the second set with 45 pushups, the third set with 55 pushups and end it with the last set of 70 pushups. The time interval between the sets
was 60 seconds between the first and second set, 90 seconds between the second and third set, and 120 seconds between the last 2 sets.
Most of the time I would not warm up and just start a full blast, I would go all 4 sets with 60 pushups with at most 90 seconds break in between.

It is a pretty intensive short-duration training if you follow it.

<h3>SO HOW DID I DO 600 PUSHUPS??</h3>

Soon after my pushups stamina increased dramatically, I did following:
<ul>
    <li> Start a timer.</li>
    <li> Immediately start pushups. Do 50 of them.</li>
    <li> Stop after 50 pushup.</li>
    <li> Rest until timer hits 5 minute.</li>
    <li> Start pushups when timer hits 5 minute. Complete 50 pushups.</li>
    <li> Rest until timer hits 10 minute.</li>
    <li> Start pushups when timer hits 10 minute. Complete 50 pushups.</li>
    <li> Continue this for an hour.</li>
</ul>

Notice that I don't stop the timer. The longer I take to complete 50 pushups, the less rest I get between sets.

The more I did, the simpler it got to complete 600 pushups. Eventually, I went on to mix squats, barbell rows, biceps curl, and abs-roll-out in between the sets. You can try any of the ways to work out.

<h3> PERSISTENCE IS THE KEY!</h3>
