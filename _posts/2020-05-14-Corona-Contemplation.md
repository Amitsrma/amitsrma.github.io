I think we all can agree that our ideas and/or beliefs are the outcome of the interactions we have with our surroundings. Information we come across has a great effect in determining what we think is correct. I would like to present a case, an optimistic one, on coronavirus that got enormous attention because of bias we developed through the information presented to us. It is entirely possible that I, still do not have the entire picture and I might have missed the bigger picture but I would like to present a summary of how I thought the situation evolved.

<img src="https://images.pexels.com/photos/3951628/pexels-photo-3951628.jpeg?auto=compress&cs=tinysrgb&dpr=2&h=650&w=940">

We all have some ideas about the evolution of the crisis. It started from the bat in China, China locked itself down then followed the world. There were significant deaths in China, followed by Italy, Spain, USA, UK which led governments to act and made them lock the country down. People were, and to this date are, asked to keep a distance from each other, avoid physical contact and go out of their house only if necessary.

Some reasons that led for panic to not sit right with me are:
<ol>
    <li>Asymptomatic Carriers.</li>
    <li>Unavailability of testing kits.</li>
    <li>Panic on case discovery.</li>
    <li>Death and perception of Death.</li>
    <li>Reporting Mechanism.</li>
</ol>

This virus is new and methods for properly testing the COVID-19 virus are not known. This caused paucity of testing kits, they were not available when they were most required. Now, how can infection be found if there are no ways to determine there has been an occurrence. Quarantine was applied long after China started quarantine. Transportation was free all around the world until February. It was not until the first week of March when feeling for lockdown and travel restrictions started to feel like the right decision. Considering the global mobility and how effectively the virus could spread, a lot of people might have been already affected by it.

When people started getting themselves tested, some got positive results, some got negative. It was a relief for those who got negative but those with positive results seemed like odd cases (obviously, because the virus is new). Especially because the virus was asymptomatic and could remain so for up to 2 weeks. This means a person could infect as many people they come in contact with for 2 weeks and feel something that would make them go get tested. And we have to be aware that we were not conscious of the concept of physical distancing at the moment. This simple mechanism, asymptomatic carrier, points to the fact that there should be many people who have been affected by it. This can be vindicated by the post-lockdown infection numbers that kept coming.

This was followed by the statistics of death. I remember people being placed in ventilators because the virus seemed to affect the lungs and its capacity. The practice of placing people on a ventilator was revisited after it came to light that this process was harming the patients. This created bias as to what the virus can do. The virus seems to badly affect the people with already severe health conditions, it makes the most sense to assume that people with severe health conditions go for testing. If the people with already severe health conditions undergo complications, they end up living final moments of their life on the hospital bed. But this would be a small fraction of a population that was infected. Because the symptoms of the virus are similar to that of flu, it is possible that many did not test assuming it was flu and those who got better should already have immunity against the virus. This leads to a simple conclusion, there should already be herd immunity in many places, we just don't know it yet.

We know that a huge number of people breathe their last breath because of the flu. We know the cases of a person suffering from AIDS can develop severe flu and end up dying from it. In this case, we cannot say that the flu was the reason for that person's death, it was AIDS. With the enormous attention that coronavirus has got, I have not read the reports, coronavirus may have been wrongly accused of costing the lives of people when the real culprit might be some other health condition or conditions.

In addition to that, when a person is tested for coronavirus, reports take a certain number of days (4-9 days) to be available. So, when we see a surge in virus cases on Thursday, what it means is a bunch of people tested some days ago and some of their reports came today. It could mean that testing done 4-9 days ago could have come today.

This could all be regarded as speculation but data seem to point in the same direction. It is only a matter of time when we will see the actual severity and effect of virus.<br><br>
